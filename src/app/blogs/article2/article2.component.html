Investing in NFO

One of my friend came telling me “Hey, I am gonna invest in ‘…NFO ‘.She was so convinced with one point that she will be investing in a mutual fund offer with the lowest NAV .”

Actually the problem is people generally compare NFO with IPO. When a company brings its IPO, it is already in existence and engaged in operations, and therefore it gives investors an idea of the fundamental strengths and its performance. It would be like shooting in the dark. You never know where the bullet will aim. However , there is no doubt that blind bet can be extremely successful, but the risk of failure is also extremely high.

But on the other side, in an NFO, investors have nothing to evaluate. Since the fund is new , it has no record which can be used to study its performance or growth. Even if the fund manager’s performance is known and he/she may be managing other schemes in the similar category, yet the scheme objective may be different & so its performance.

NFO is generally misunderstood concept. A New Fund Offer (NFO) occurs when an investment company launches a new fund on a first-subscription basis for financing its purchase of securities. Generally AMCs come up with NFO’s when the markets are bullish & the themes are re bundled products. In a NFO the opportunity to subscribe to the scheme is available only for a Limited period. The investors may subscribe to the NFO at given offer price which is generally fixed at Rs. 10.

Before investing one should look for Own Risk profile, Fund Objective, Theme of the Fund, Risk Factor, Cost of investment, Investment horizon, lock-in periods etc.

One of the important factor is Expense Ratio. Expense Ratios are on higher sides during an NFO and also in initial years as well. Does that mean initially the NFO investor’s money is used for all initial marketing and advertising? Well, I think investors should look at this.*/TER which is cost of investment to investor and not for low NAVs .If this expense ratio is same for existing schemes with consistent return record then investing in a new and with no past track record scheme will not make sense.

Think before investing in NFO if it really has positive space in your portfolio.
